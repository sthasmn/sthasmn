Conference Justifications

LREC-COLING 2026: Language Resources and Evaluation Conference

Strategic Justification: This is the premier international forum for our exact type of contribution. The conference's core mission is the development, dissemination, and evaluation of language resources. Unlike other venues where a dataset is a secondary contribution, at LREC, our novel Japanese corpus, recording pipeline, and alignment backend are the primary focus and will be judged on their scientific merit and utility to the community. The "Language Resource Development" and "Multimodality" tracks are a perfect fit, making this our highest-probability venue for acceptance and impact within the language technology field.

CVPR 2026: Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition

Strategic Justification: As the top-tier conference in computer vision, CVPR offers unparalleled prestige and visibility. Crucially, it has a dedicated "Datasets and evaluation" track, signaling that the community values high-quality corpus contributions as first-class research. Our work also aligns perfectly with the "Humans: Face, body, pose, gesture, movement" topic. To succeed here, we must frame the paper as a comprehensive benchmark, complete with strong baseline model performance and rigorous analysis of the corpus. This is an ideal venue to establish our dataset as a standard for future Japanese lip-reading research.

FG 2026: IEEE International Conference on Automatic Face and Gesture Recognition

Strategic Justification: FG is the premier specialized conference for research in face and gesture recognition, making it a highly targeted and relevant venue. Lip-reading is a core task within this community. Submitting here ensures our work will be reviewed and seen by the leading experts in this specific subfield. The conference's location in Kyoto, Japan, also presents a unique opportunity to engage directly with the local research community that would benefit most from a Japanese-language corpus. The two-round submission system offers valuable flexibility.

ICIVP 2026: International Conference on Image and Vision Processing

Strategic Justification: ICIVP offers a focused, international forum for core research in image and vision processing. While not as large as CVPR or ECCV, it provides a valuable platform to present detailed technical work to a dedicated audience. This venue is ideal for highlighting the specifics of your recording pipeline and alignment backend. Its strong track record of being held in Asia, including Japan, makes it an excellent conference for networking with regional experts and institutions that would be primary users of a high-quality Japanese lip-reading corpus.

ECCV 2026: European Conference on Computer Vision

Strategic Justification: ECCV is another of the top three global computer vision conferences, on par with CVPR in terms of prestige and impact. It shares a similar scope and also values dataset contributions. The later submission deadline in March 2026 provides a valuable alternative to CVPR, allowing more time for paper refinement and experimentation. The strategic positioning is identical to CVPR: frame the work as a new, challenging benchmark for the computer vision community.

ACL 2026: Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics

Strategic Justification: As the leading conference in NLP, ACL provides an opportunity to frame our work for a different, highly relevant audience. Here, the paper should be positioned as a contribution to multimodal language understanding—specifically, the creation of a novel parallel corpus (video-to-text) for a "visual language." The "Resources and Evaluation" and "Multimodality and Language Grounding" tracks are directly applicable. Acceptance at ACL would highlight the linguistic value of our corpus and its potential to advance research beyond pure computer vision.

ICLR 2026: International Conference on Learning Representations

Strategic Justification: ICLR is a top-tier machine learning conference focused on deep learning and representation learning. This is a high-risk, high-reward venue. A submission is only viable if we can strongly emphasize a novel machine learning contribution, such as a new architecture in our alignment backend or a new learning technique for the lip-reading model itself. The dataset alone would be insufficient. However, if we have a significant methodological innovation, acceptance at ICLR would signal its broad applicability to the wider ML community.
